The Rules That Solve the Problem

✅ APM/PM owns the Pre-Refinement → Needs Refinement → Ready flow
›Stories do not move to Ready until sized, prioritized, and ownership confirmed at a Refinement meeting.
›SM (Nancy) facilitates the Refinement ceremony. Needs Refinement bucket = the agenda. Ready bucket = the output.
›If AC cannot be completed without a dependency answer → story stays in Team Refinement with a dependency tag and a date. PM/APM owns AC resolution — escalating to SM is not the first step.
📅 PM/APM uses capacity view to target a sprint
›Once a story hits Ready, PM/APM reviews the Q2 capacity table below and identifies the earliest open sprint.
›"I don't know which sprint" is not acceptable at Ready stage — use the table.
›SM (Nancy) confirms capacity at Sprint Planning. PM/APM does not need SM to assign sprint targets for Ready items.
🔄 Stories move BACK out of the sprint when blocked or incomplete
›If a story cannot be completed this sprint due to a missing dependency → it moves back to Hold in the backlog.
›The sprint is a dev execution container for Richard — not a PM/SPM/APM holding area.
›PM/APM re-pulls the story in a future sprint once resolved. This is expected behavior — not a failure.
🚫 APM/PM product work does not live in the sprint
›PM (Emily, Sam) · APM (Maddy) work lives in Backlog, Pre-Refinement, and Needs Refinement buckets. Product work does not get put into Sprints.
›Stories reach Ready only after moving through Pre-Refinement → Needs Refinement → sized and prioritized at Refinement ceremony.
›If a story requires PM/SPM/APM action to complete → move it to Hold. It does not belong in the sprint.
